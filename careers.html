Careers & Opportunities

Our global chapters recruit like-minded teammates year-round. Whether you are a seasoned professional or a passionate student, this is where your talent becomes real social impact.

Program Coordinator

Full-time / Part-time Los Angeles HQ / Hybrid

Responsibilities:
Run day-to-day operations for the Youth Cabinet and the Stage I–IV Program; coordinate branch activities across time zones; maintain partnerships with academic institutions and sponsors.

Requirements:
Bachelor's degree or above, preferably in sociology, psychology, or management; excellent bilingual (Chinese/English) communication; experience with NGOs, student organizations, or cross-border project management.

Mental Health Research Assistant

Internship / Project-based Remote (Global)

Responsibilities:
Assist the Research Director in collecting, cleaning, and analyzing field data; help draft academic papers, white papers, and survey designs on Chinese community mental wellbeing.

Requirements:
Current student or recent graduate in psychology, statistics, public health, or related fields; proficiency in SPSS/R/Python; rigorous academic mindset and strong literature-research skills.

Cross-Cultural Communications Specialist

Volunteer / Part-time Remote (Global)

Responsibilities:
Plan and design content for CNPAF's WeChat, RED, and Instagram channels; write press releases and feature interviews to grow our social impact.

Requirements:
Sharp digital instincts and strong writing — able to explain complex psychology in plain, warm language; fluent with layout and design tools; deeply aligned with our de-pathologizing philosophy.

How to Apply

If any of these roles interests you — or you believe your unique skills could create irreplaceable value for CNPAF — send your résumé in English and Chinese (with portfolio or research experience) to our recruiting inbox.

Subject line: "Position — Your Name"

hr@cnpaf.org

We contact shortlisted candidates within 3–5 business days.

华人心理援助基金会 CHINESE PSYCHOLOGICAL ASSISTANCE FOUNDATION

The Chinese Psychological Assistance Foundation (CNPAF) is a nonprofit dedicated to culturally attuned, mentorship-driven support for overseas Chinese communities. We assume every visitor is "not ill" — filling the systemic gap between struggling alone and clinical treatment. We are proudly included in the UN DESA Civil Society Database.
